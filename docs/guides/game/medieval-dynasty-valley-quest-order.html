The short answer

Advance Chapters as soon as your settlement can absorb their requirements; advance Alwin whenever his next stage appears; complete Dobroniega early; let Uniegost run in parallel; and do not trigger Uniegost’s final conversation until every Dobroniega objective is finished.

Do now

Chapters I–VII

These unlock development stages and building capacity. They are the mechanical spine of the playthrough.

Time-sensitive

Alwin’s Story I

Complete the opening stage well before Year 6. Starting it at the last moment is needlessly risky.

Protect first

Dobroniega I–II

Finish both before Uniegost VII. This is the one story dependency that deserves a hard checkpoint.

Safe to pace

Uniegost and Sambor

Their opening windows are generous—18 and 17 years respectively—but doing them early makes the travel useful rather than tedious.

Always trust the Journal. Patch changes and custom settings can outlive guides. Before every season change, check active quest timers. Ordinary seasonal side quests usually expire at season end; Chapters and the fixed Story chains use different rules.

How to maximize Dynasty Reputation

Dynasty Reputation is primarily a population gate: more reputation lets you recruit more adults as the settlement grows. It is separate from development stage, technology points, Diplomacy experience, and the King’s favor—even though some activities advance more than one of them.

10,190 base points from the prologue and all ten listed Chapter rewards combined, before the Diplomat skill or a custom Dynasty Reputation multiplier. This is why Chapter completion beats aimless grinding.
Best multiplier

Take Diplomat early

The Tier-1 Diplomacy skill grants 20% / 40% / 60% more Dynasty Reputation. If min-maxing, put points here before completing the final objective of high-value Chapters—especially VI through X.

Repeatable source

Clear safe side quests each season

New side quests appear every season and commonly award reputation. Batch nearby errands, but accept only work you can finish before the season changes.

Two rewards, two systems

Use Herald Challenges selectively

Challenges can award Dynasty Reputation and King’s favor. They normally allow five seasons, including the acceptance season. King’s favor can reset when the ruler changes; your Dynasty Reputation does not.

Indirect setup

Trade to level Diplomacy

Selling and buying do not directly grant Dynasty Reputation. They grant Diplomacy experience, which helps unlock and rank Diplomat before the large quest rewards arrive.

Reputation gotchas

Gotcha What actually happens Best practice
Chapter rewards auto-trigger A Chapter completes as soon as its last condition is satisfied; there is no separate “turn in” moment at which to remember Diplomat. Spend the Diplomacy point before recruiting the final resident, killing the final animal, or crossing the final reputation threshold.
Failed side quests cost reputation Accepted ordinary side quests expire at season change and apply a small loss when unfinished. Unaccepted quests simply disappear. Read the objective and remaining days before accepting. Finish active quests before sleeping into a new season.
Witnessed theft costs reputation A red Steal prompt is not free loot. If an NPC sees the theft, reputation loss scales with the stolen item’s value. For a clean maximum-reputation run, never use a red prompt. A handful of planks is not worth sabotaging a recruitment threshold.
Tax debt drains reputation Failing the spring tax quest converts tax into debt and reduces Dynasty Reputation over time. Reserve the tax amount during winter and pay in spring before accepting distant errands.
King’s favor is not Dynasty Reputation The Map’s ruler standing affects taxes and unlocks royal benefits, but it is a separate score that can reset when the King changes. Do Herald work for both rewards, but do not read high royal favor as extra recruitment capacity.
Building does not directly grind reputation Construction, crafting, hunting, and selling only award reputation when they satisfy a quest or Chapter objective. Choose activities that overlap an active objective; otherwise you are earning resources, XP, or technology—not Dynasty Reputation.
Children are treated differently Children do not count against the adult recruitment calculation while young; they begin mattering when they mature. Keep a reputation cushion before a large cohort reaches adulthood instead of recruiting right to the current limit.
The custom multiplier changes the race Gameplay settings include a Dynasty Reputation gain multiplier and can be changed independently of XP and technology gains. Leave it at 100% for a vanilla comparison; raise it deliberately if you want a large settlement without seasonal quest grinding.
Practical min-max route: complete the tiny early Chapters normally; trade and finish the three side quests required by Chapter III; invest in Diplomat as Diplomacy points arrive; then make sure the perk is active before Chapters VI–X. At maximum rank, its description promises 60% more reputation, making each late Chapter disproportionately valuable.
Do not chase a stale threshold chart. Recruitment requirements have changed across versions. Use the live Management panel to see whether reputation is currently blocking another adult recruit.

Recommended route

The windows below are milestones, not strict calendar dates. “Opening year” means your first four seasons; a relaxed player can move the middle stages into Year 2 without harming the route.

Starting a New Life

Opening day · complete immediately

Speak to Uniegost in Gostovia. This prologue grants permission to settle and unlocks Chapter I. There is no advantage to wandering first.

Chapter I — A New Beginning

Opening day or two

Gather the listed materials, craft the axe and hammer, cut three trees, and build the first house. Choose your intended settlement region now, but treat the first house as movable infrastructure rather than a sacred monument.

Open all three early story routes

First spring · after the first house

  • Alwin I: give Dalebor a stick, report back, then wait for the next summer. Do this now; its opening deadline is six years.
  • Uniegost I: begin the ale, Sambor, and clay sequence once you have food and a few weapons.
  • Dobroniega I: accept it during Uniegost I and investigate the rye delivery.

At Sambor’s clay pits, pick up at least one wooden deer figurine and ask him about it. That deliberately unlocks Sambor I.

Chapter II — A Survivalist

First season · let the season finish

Set the rabbit trap, craft a spear and knife, and hunt one animal. The chapter also requires surviving a season, so use the remaining days for food, firewood, reeds, and the story errands above.

Chapter III — Good Morning My Neighbours

Second season · finish before taking long trips

Complete three current side quests, spend a skill point, and flirt successfully. Accept only side quests you can finish before the season turns. These errands also begin building Diplomacy experience; prioritize Diplomat if maximum Dynasty Reputation is your goal. This raises the building limit to 10.

Alwin II, then Dobroniega II

As soon as each appears

Alwin II combines a waterskin delivery with hunting and leather; finish it when summer activates it, then observe its one-day wait. Dobroniega II sends you between Rolnica and the road to Gostovia; finish it now so the entire Dobroniega chain is permanently safe.

Chapters IV–V — Into the Wilderness and A Farm

Opening year · in a valid sowing season

Build a Hunting Lodge and hunt three foxes or boars. Then acquire the hoe, bag, fertilizer, and requested seed stocks; place, grub, fertilize, plough, and sow a field. Check the crop calendar before consuming seed—Chapter V is easiest in spring or summer, when several requested crops can be sown.

Sambor I–III

Opening year or Year 2 · complete as one travel block

Visit Fenenna, then Norbert, assemble the unusual herb-and-manure delivery, wait a day, and finish the Bogdan/Fenenna resolution before returning to Sambor. The chain has a generous 17-year window, but early completion saves repeated cross-map journeys.

Uniegost II–IV

Opening year through Year 2 · whenever unlocked

  • II: recover the scythe and resolve Ida’s riddles.
  • III: investigate Sambor’s “owl,” return, and observe the one-day wait.
  • IV: take the parcel to Wolrad and follow the conversations. The 1,000-log demand is a joke/setup; follow the dialogue rather than harvesting a small forest.

Chapter VI — The Resourcefulness

Late opening year or Year 2 · before serious recruitment

Build the Barn, Resource Storage, Woodshed, Food Storage, Well, and Workshop, and reach 500 reputation. These are useful foundations, not filler. Equip your available Diplomat ranks before placing the final building or crossing 500 reputation; this Chapter’s 500-point reward is the first substantial multiplier opportunity. Completion raises the building limit to 25.

Chapter VII — Starting a Community

Year 1–2 · only after food, water, and firewood are stable

Reach 15 buildings, provide two suitable homes, and recruit two residents. Assign productive work immediately—typically extraction and food or farming—rather than creating two new dependants without a supply chain. Set Diplomat before inviting the second resident, because that final invitation can instantly trigger the 750-point Chapter reward.

Alwin III–VII

Year 1–3 · advance every time the wait clears

Keep 120 coins for III. IV is a guided trip to the bow seller; V needs five logs and a wooden hammer; VI and VII are archery challenges separated by season waits. Save before the shooting stages if ammunition or score pressure would spoil the fun.

Uniegost V–VI and Angler Without a Cause

Year 2–3 · after the farm and cash flow exist

Prepare 20 each of beetroot, onion, cabbage, and carrot plus a Potion of Sobriety for V. For VI, keep 500 coins, 20 dried meat, and a filled waterskin ready. Complete Angler Without a Cause when the crossbow problem appears; it is an embedded detour, not a reason to abandon the chain.

Chapter VIII — A Big Game

Year 2–3 · once you own a bow and reliable arrows

Hunt one moose, one wisent, and one bear. Bring healing, spare ammunition, and a knife. Activate Diplomat before the last required kill to multiply the 1,000-point reward. If Alwin VIII is active, both objectives involve a bear; pursue them in the same expedition, while checking each tracker afterward rather than assuming one kill credited both.

Alwin VIII–IX

By Year 3–5 · comfortably before the opening deadline becomes relevant

Hunt the bear for VIII. For IX, speak with Gizela and Alwin and obtain the requested recurve bow. This closes Alwin’s fixed story. The safe habit is to finish the chain once started rather than remembering the Year 6 deadline in a panic.

Chapter IX — Newcomers

Year 2–4 · after your stores stay positive for a full season

Reach 10 inhabitants and employ at least five workers. Recruit for actual vacancies, add housing first, and account for mothers temporarily leaving the workforce after childbirth. Have Diplomat active before the last recruit or fifth work assignment completes the Chapter: its base reward is 2,500 points.

Uniegost VII — final checkpoint

After Dobroniega II · save before the final conversation

Hard stop: verify that Dobroniega’s story is complete before speaking to Uniegost at the end. The finale changes Gostovia’s NPC roster and can fail unfinished Dobroniega content.

Speak to Dyter, then confront Uniegost. Both final outcomes grant the same reputation, so choose for role-play rather than optimization.

Chapter X — The Dynasty Continues

Long-term · begin the family requirement early, finish naturally

Have a wife, have an heir, and reach 5,000 reputation. Pregnancy takes three seasons. Court someone during the early settlement years, but do not rush marriage solely for the checkbox; spouse personality and skills matter for the longer playthrough. Keep Diplomat active when the last condition resolves—the Chapter itself has a 5,000-point base reward.

Conditional spouse and heir stories

These are also categorized as Valley Story Quests, but they depend on marriage, pregnancy, the heir’s age, personality, or a spouse-generated opportunity. They cannot be placed into one universal Chapter order. The ideal rule is simple: complete them in the season they are offered unless the Journal explicitly grants longer.

Annual housekeeping that is not part of the fixed story order

Pay Taxes appears every spring. Pay the castellan—or ask your wife to handle it—before debt and reputation penalties accumulate. At every season end, finish or consciously abandon ordinary side quests, then review food, water, firewood, tools, worker assignments, and active quest timers.
